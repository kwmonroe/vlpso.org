The Classical Approach

Classical education is a time-tested educational model rooted in the traditions of Western culture. It emphasizes the seeking of truth, goodness, and beauty, and the cultivation of wisdom and virtue through a rigorous study of the liberal arts and sciences.

The Trivium

The core pedagogical structure is often the Trivium, which aligns with a child's natural cognitive development:

  • Grammar Stage (Grades K-6): Focuses on foundational knowledge â€“ the building blocks of subjects. Memorization of facts, rules of phonics, spelling, grammar, math facts, historical dates, scientific terms, etc.
  • Logic Stage (Grades 7-9): Focuses on reasoning and critical thinking. Students learn to analyze arguments, detect fallacies, and understand cause-and-effect relationships across subjects. Formal logic is often studied.
  • Rhetoric Stage (Grades 10-12): Focuses on effective and eloquent communication. Students learn to synthesize information, construct persuasive arguments, and express their ideas clearly and beautifully, both in writing and speech.

Why It Matters

This approach aims not just to impart information, but to forge students who know how to learn, think critically, and communicate effectively. It connects students to the "Great Conversation" of Western thought through classic literature, primary sources, and the study of foundational languages like Latin.

Supporting teachers in this tradition helps provide the resources needed to make this rich educational experience possible.
